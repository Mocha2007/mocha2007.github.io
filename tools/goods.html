Values

Values are given in an equivalent weight of silver unless otherwise specified. eg. "GOLD = 12:1" means 12 pounds of silver is worth 1 lb of gold. "PEPPER = 1:8" means 1 lb of silver is worth 8 lbs of pepper.

Methodology for CoL/SoL

I base cost of living on a diet consisting of the CHEAPEST goods in the following categories:

  • Grain: 500g (fallback: any good)
  • Dairy: 485g (fallback: grain, then any good)
  • Vegetables: 300g (fallback: grain, then any good)
  • Fruit: 200g (fallback: vegetable, then grain, then any good)
  • Meat (incl. Poultry): 60g (fallback: grain, then any good)
  • Nuts: 25g (fallback: dairy, then grain, then any good)
  • Fish: 20g (fallback: meat, then grain, then any good)
  • Fabric: 10g (proxy for clothing; fallback: any good)

By dividing the wage by this CoL figure, we can establish a rough estimate with which we can compare disposable income.
