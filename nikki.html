There are short-long pairs for each vowel. Schwa is considered the short vowel component of the central and back long vowels. Formerly these were /a ɔ ʊ/, but they have since merged. Spelling has not updated to reflect this.

Grammar

Morphology

  • 2 Categories of Inflection per verb - tense and class - number collapsed for past tense.
  • Two classes - inanimate singular and animate/plural
  • Primarily prefixed; the only suffixes are for declension and conjugation.
  • Adjectives, nouns, and pronouns decline to number. Most pronouns and determiners also decline to case.

Syntax

  • Marked nominative
  • primarily SVO; questions, imperatives, negatives, conditionals are VSO
  • Prepositional
  • Most adjectives can come either before or after the noun; [PREP DEM NUM (ADJ)] NOUN [(ADJ) GEN REL]
  • Adv - Adj
  • Negation marked by special verb derived from BE
  • Questions are marked solely by word order and are distinguished only from conditional clauses in that no effect clause follows
