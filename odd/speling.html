Speling Ruform

Okay, I'll admit... this is becoming more of a language and less of a spelling convention. For example, the horrendous yet accurate lengwudj.

Vowels

Vowel IPA Example Ugzampul
Aa /æ/ ash ac
Āā /ɑ/ all āl
Ee /ɛ/ elk elk
Ēē /e/ age ēdj
Ii /ɪ/ itch itc
Īī /i/ east īst
Oo /ʌ/ hum hom
Ōō /o/ oak ōk
Uu /ʊ~ə/ him* hum
Ūū /u/ whom hūm
* - unstressed

Word-finally, and before R or NG, unmacronized vowels represent the sound of their macronized forms, except u/ū - eg. tre represents /e/, not /ɛ/, and er represents /e/, not /ɛ/. The only exception to this is ONG, which represents /ʌŋ/ as expected. Additionally, OL represents /ol/, not /ʌl/.
