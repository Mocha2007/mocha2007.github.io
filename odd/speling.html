Speling Ruform

Okay, I'll admit... this is becoming more of a language and less of a spelling convention. For example, the horrendous yet accurate lengwudj.

Vowels

Vowel IPA Example Ugzampul
Aa /æ/ bat bat
Āā /ɑ/ bot bāt
Ee /ɛ/ bet bet
Ēē /e/ bait bēt
Ii /ɪ/ bit bit
Īī /i/ beat bīt
Oo /ʌ/ but bot
Ōō /o/ boat bōt
Uu /ʊ~ə/ but* but
Ūū /u/ boot būt
* - unstressed

Word-finally, and before R or NG, unmacronized vowels represent the sound of their macronized forms, except u/ū - eg. tre represents /e/, not /ɛ/, and er represents /e/, not /ɛ/. The only exception to this is ONG, which represents /ʌŋ/ as expected.
