Porön Language

Porön is the language spoken in Porön, a region north of the Muran languages.

Phonology

There are 26 consonants and 13 vowels in Porön. The stress in words always lies on the first syllable. Sentence-level stress tends to be trochaic (high-low).

N-Mutation

N-mutation mutates consonants to their corresponding nasal of the same place of articulation, and nasalizes vowels. For /h/, it simply becomes /n/.

Morphology

  • The plural in nouns and adjectives is formed by y-mutation of the last consonant, and verbs are pluralized by n-mutation of the last consonant.
  • Neither nouns nor pronouns decline to case.

Affixes

  • -Y = forms the plural of most nouns and adjectives
  • -n = is a "collection" affix affixed to plural nouns, treated grammatically singular
  • -sac = similar to Esperanto -uj- and -ing-
  • -tai = aggrandizer; "big", cf. eo. -eg-
  • -hän = forms adjectives from nouns

Grammar

  • Verbs conjugate to person of subject and object, aspect, mood, volition, and evidentiality.
  • There exists a definite but no indefinite article
  • VSOX
  • Prepositional
  • [Prep] [Dem] [Num] Noun [Adj] [Gen] [Rel]
  • [Adv] Adj/Verb
  • Auxiliary verbs precede the verb they modify
  • Question particles are placed after the verb (in the second position of the clause)
