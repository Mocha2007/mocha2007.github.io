Važcud Language

Važcud (IPA: ['vaʒ.t͡ʃud]) is the language spoken in Važcud, a late bronze age society that ruled the east of Pankair at the height of the last era.

Phonology

There are 24 consonants and 10 vowels in Važcud. Stress is always on the first syllable. There are five vowels: /a e i o u/. Each vowel can be nasalized or clear. The consonants are as follows:

Syntax

  • SOXV word order default.
  • ADP DET NUM ADJ noun GEN RELC default, but any arrangement is possible
  • much like english, there is a distinction between "don't want to see" and "want to not see": zogusakuwe vs. zoguwasal
  • subordinators (ie. if, when, because, etc. are clause-initial)
  • Reduplication:
    • partial reduplication (onset+nucleus of first syll doubled) is used for intensive verbs, eg. shine -> shimmer, but is not used anywheres else
    • full reduplication is used with nouns to mean "lots of", and with verbs to mean "all the time, often, constantly"
  • Unique relative and interrogative pronouns
