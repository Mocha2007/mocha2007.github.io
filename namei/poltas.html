Półtaś Language

Półtaś is the language spoken in Półtaś.

Phonotactics

  • Maximal onset is Fricative - Nasal/Plosive/Affricate - Approximant/Tap
  • Maximal coda is the reverse
  • Sonorants can be nuclei
  • (C)(C)(C)V(C)(C)(C)
  • /dgkɫnstxz/ cannot exist before front vowels
  • front vowels and back vowels cannot coexist in the same word; any stragglers match the backness of the head

Grammar

  • OVS order is the default, but changes to VOS to form questions.
  • Adjectives (incl. demonstratives and numerals) and genitives precede nouns.
  • Adpositions come immediately after the noun they modify.
  • There are two genders: masculine and feminine
  • There are fifteen cases, but the bare nouns themselves just decline to two.
  • Only demonstratives decline for case and gender.
  • Some masculine nouns decline for genitives special (-ei), and some feminine (-i), rest use the default genitive-acceptor (-y)
  • Numerals are nouns.

Gender

Unlike in most European languages, gender assignment is not random, always semantic.

Masculine

  • Beings
    • Male animals, incl. men
    • Large animals/plants of unknown or inapplicable sex
    • Insects
  • Most tools and instruments
  • The Sun, day
  • Fire

Feminine

  • Beings
    • Female animals, incl. women
    • Small animals/plants of unknown or inapplicable sex
  • Vehicles
  • Precipitation
  • Bodies of water
  • Celestial bodies (other than the sun), night
  • Most abstractions
