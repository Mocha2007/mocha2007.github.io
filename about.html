There were numerous known routes where Trump could win - eg. "secret trump voters", the so-called "blue wall", which speaking of which...

The blue wall was always unstable.

Based on polling data from before the election, each blue wall state was the equivalent of rolling one six-sided die... and hoping to not roll a one or two. Now, granted, most of the dice won't be ones... but it'd be more surprising if one or two didn't flip. Keep in mind, the blue wall consisted of up to seven states (depending on who you ask), so we would expect two or three to flip on average (in the actual election, five did). Statistically speaking, the results of the election were higher than the average, but not obscenely so.
