Fantasy Races

Fantasy Races.

Elves
Sometimes portrayed as being smaller than humans, other times portrayed as the same size. They often have pointy ears. They tend to be associated with purity and magic. Often good. Sometimes subdivided into separate classes, eg. "High Elves", "Dark Elves", and "Wood Elves".
D&D, Dwarf Fortress, LotR, TES
Demon
Usually portrayed as satyrs with red skin and horns, taller than humans. Often wielding a pitchfork and nearly always wearing nothing more than a loincloth. Nearly universally evil.
Dwarf Fortress, Terraria
Drow
Synonymous with dark elves. Their skintone ranges from blue to purple to black. Usually neutral, sometimes evil instead.
Dwarf
Dwarves are shorter versions of humans which tend to live in mountains. Often depicted with long beards, thick metal armor, and wielding axes or warhammers. Fairly neutral.
Dwarf Fortress, TES
Ettin
Depicted either identically to giants, or more often as two-headed giants. Usually evil.
Dwarf Fortress
Giant
Depicted in many ways similarly to orcs and ogres, but closer to humans, often with blue or grey skin. Depiction of alignment varies, but often neutral or evil.
Dwarf Fortress, TES
Gnoll
Antropomorphic hyenas. Often evil.
Gnome
Usually depicted as short versions of elves, much as halflings are depicted as the short versions of humans. Neutral.
Dwarf Fortress
Goblin
Usually depicted as short, hairless, long-eared versions of humans, ranging in color from green (most common) to purple. Often depicted with abnormally long or short noses. Usually wearing little more than a loincloth. Nearly universally evil.
Dwarf Fortress, TES
Kobold
Usually depicted similarly to goblins, but instead ranging in color from purple to orange, and often more beastlike. Nearly universally evil.
Dwarf Fortress
Halfling
Often depicted as just small humans. Neutral.
Hobbit
Sometimes contrasted with, but usually synonymous with halflings. Neutral.
Hobgoblin
Generally depicted as similar to but distinct from goblins. Consequently tends to be evil.
Human

Minotaur
Depicted as humans with the legs and head of a bull. Often depicted as being taller than humans, but usually by less than a meter. If wielding a weapon, it is usually a club or an axe. Typically depicted wearing either just a loincloth or minimal leather armor. Usually evil.
Dwarf Fortress, Terraria
Nymph
Depicted as fair humans, almost always female. Depiction of alignment varies wildly, can be good, neutral, or evil.
Terraria
Ogre
Depicted similarly to orcs, but fatter, and less likely to have tattoos or piercings. Usually evil.
Dwarf Fortress
Orc
Often depicted as large, muscular, green versions of humans. Often depicted with large lower incisors. Often depicted with piercings and tattoos. Occasionally depicted as being purple or red instead of green. Usually evil, sometimes neutral instead.
TES
Satyr
Humans with the legs (and rarely, the head) of goats. Usually wearing little more than a loincloth, if anything, but certainly no more than leather armor. If wielding a weapon, it is a bow. For historical reasons, they are associated with flutes and fertility. Depiction of alignment varies wildly, can be good, neutral, or evil.
Dwarf Fortress
Troll
Depicted nearly indistinguishably from ogres, except usually grey in skin. Usually evil.
Dwarf Fortress, TES
