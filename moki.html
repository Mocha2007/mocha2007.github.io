Introduction

Moki is a computationally universal programming language. After dissatisfaction with my first language, Mochine, I've since turned to more golfy methods of representation. In this aspect I've made as many functions as string-compatible as possible. Multiplication, division, and even exponentiation work with strings. Instead of erroring out I try and think of alternative uses until the logic simply breaks down and I have to issue an error. The only errors, currently, are dividing by zero, raising zero to a non-positive number, raising a negative to a noninteger (I'll add complex math soon!~), mismatching braces, and using unindexed operations.
