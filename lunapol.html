LunaPol

The idea behind this document is a sort of hypothetical "what-if" - if I could, with the snap of a finger, magically transform the function of the US into what I think would be most ideal, regardless of how major or trivial or improbable the change, what would that ideal look like?

No detail is too small, no change is too big to escape this loment.

Federal Holidays

At the time of writing, the US has 11 federal holidays. Let's go over the list one-by-one.

  • 1 Jan - New Year's
    • No change.
  • 3rd Monday in Jan - Martin Luther King Jr. Day
    • No change.
  • 4th Monday in Feb - Washington's Birthday
    • Move from the third monday to the fourth Monday, so it actually aligns with his actual birthday.
  • 1st Monday in May - Labor day
    • Move from the first Monday in September to the first Monday in May.
  • Last Monday in May - Memorial day
    • No change.
  • 3rd Monday in Jun - Juneteenth
    • Move from a fixed date of 19 Jun to a floating Monday (15th-21st), so there's always a three-day weekend.
  • 4 Jul - Independence Day
    • No change.
  • 2nd Monday in Oct - Indigenous People's Day
    • Rename (many states already do this).
  • 1st Monday in Nov - [NEW] Election Day
    • Long been suggested to be a holiday. Move to a floating Monday, so there's always a three-day weekend.
  • 2nd Monday in Nov - Veteran's Day
    • Move from a fixed date of 11 Nov to a floating Monday (8th-14th), so there's always a three-day weekend.
  • Last Monday in Nov - Thanksgiving
    • Move the floating date so there's always a three-day weekend.
  • 3rd Monday in Dec - Winter Solstice
    • Secularize + floating date so there's always a three-day weekend.
