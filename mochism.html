Axioms

These are the basic axioms of my philosophy. They are ordered such that values higher on the list take precedence over lower ones.

What Must Be

  1. People have the right to have neither themselves nor their property be physically contacted without consent, or used in a way against their consent.
  2. People's freedom to perform any action should never be impeded.

Note: property is and may only be any physical item - IP including patents, slogans, etc. are free game.

What Must Be Punished

  • Any violation of a person's rights as outlined above not conflicting with the following.
  • Lying to others with the intent to manipulate them into performing an action contrary to that if they knew the truth is to be punished.

What Should Be

  • Knowledge should be pursued.
  • Amicability should be pursued.
  • The improvement of humanity should be pursued.

Extra Rules for Governments

  • No discrimination will be made on the basis of inalienable personal identity (eg. Religion, Gender, etc. - note that this does not include alienable identity, including voluntary association).

Positions

Five Freedoms

The five freedoms of press, petition, religion, speech, and assembly are logically derivable by Axioms 2. As none of the freedoms require breaking Axiom 1, these rights are implicit.

CP Possession
- I know this will upset some people, but imagine the following situation: Mr. Good Samaritan comes across a CP production website by accident (mistyped a link, misclicked, whatever), and reports it to the police. The police arrest Mr. Kiddy Diddler - so far so good, right? But here's where the problem lies: Mr. Good Samaritan may have inadvertently downloaded CP onto their machine. If CP Possession is made blanket-illegal, Mr. Good Samaritan gets booked for possession. Did Mr. Good Samaritan do anything wrong? In my opinion, no. Thus, to me, possession of CP in and of itself should not always be a crime. There are also numerous other examples one can come up with, but I think this is the most agreeable.
